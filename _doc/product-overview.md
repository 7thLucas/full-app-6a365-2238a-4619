# 3D Tic-Tac-Toe *(working title)*

## What It Is
A solo browser-based game that transforms classic tic-tac-toe into an interactive 3D experience on a 3×3×3 grid. The player competes against an AI opponent; winning requires completing three in a row along any axis — horizontal, vertical, pillar, or diagonal.

## Users
Solo casual players seeking low-friction, short-burst entertainment. No multiplayer, no accounts, no social layer — pure single-player focus.

## Problem
Classic tic-tac-toe is trivially solvable and loses novelty within seconds. Players want something familiar but with enough strategic depth to sustain genuine replay interest without heavy time commitment.

## Solution
A full 3D 3×3×3 board against an AI opponent. Same simple rules, exponentially more strategic space — every game feels genuinely different. Sessions run 1–3 minutes, making it the ideal idle pastime.

## Positioning
Simple, visual, addictive. A go-to idle game for anyone who wants to flex their spatial thinking without reading a manual.

## Brand & Tone
- Clean, modern, playful but not childish
- Calm visual design — premium feel without complexity
- "One more game" loop mentality

## Core Capabilities (Day One)
- Interactive 3D 3×3×3 board rendered in the browser
- AI opponent (engaging difficulty for casual solo play)
- Win detection across all axes (rows, columns, pillars, space diagonals)
- Instant restart — zero friction between games

## Scope & Constraints
- Single-player vs AI only
- Browser-based; no backend required
- No accounts, leaderboards, or multiplayer at launch

## Strategic Principles
- Simplicity first: zero onboarding friction
- Visual impact: the 3D board is the product hook
- Short session design: complete a game in under 3 minutes
